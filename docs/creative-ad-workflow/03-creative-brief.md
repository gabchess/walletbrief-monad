# WalletBrief creative brief

Date: 2026-07-19  
Status: audience, production architecture, and concept locked; final design approval pending.

## Goal

Make lingering token approvals instantly legible without fearmongering, then show that WalletBrief turns uncertainty into one clear check and an owner-controlled action.

## Audience

Primary: crypto users who have accumulated wallets, dapps, approvals, and explorer tabs but lack a quick answer to “what can still move my tokens?”

Secondary: Monad/Spark judges evaluating usefulness, execution quality, and chain relevance.

## Single promise

See what your wallet still allows. Revoke only what you choose.

## Selected concept

**The Permission Afterparty**

An empty venue still recognizes discarded access wristbands after the people have gone. The hook is: “The dapp party ends. Some token permissions don't.” The image makes persistence visible; the live product proves the solution.

## Proof hierarchy

1. A real arbitrary address can be entered.
2. Useful Monad wallet data returns quickly.
3. Current approvals come from an indexed event path, not hundreds of sequential RPC requests.
4. A revoke is simulated before the connected owner is asked to sign.
5. The existing verified executor and successful mainnet revoke remain supporting proof, not the opening hook.

## Creative constraints

- One visual metaphor, not a generic crypto-confusion montage.
- The product must appear early enough that the film cannot be mistaken for vaporware.
- Each generated shot gets one subject action, one camera move, and one focal event.
- No generated wallet UI, addresses, token balances, explorer pages, captions, logos, or CTA text.
- Avoid persistent jewelry and continuity-fragile micro-details.
- Use hands, POV, silhouettes, props, or environmental reactions when they communicate more reliably than a recurring full-body character.
- Previsualize six frames before motion generation.
- Prototype selected motion at 720p; change one variable per retry; final 1080p only after motion approval.
- Keep typography and product motion deterministic in Remotion/HyperFrames.
- Use music and tactile sound intentionally; no stock-feeling bed chosen before the cut exists.

## Delivery

- Primary master: 1920×1080, 16:9, 30 fps for X and YouTube.
- Output stops at QA-passed master plus ready-to-post copy and links.
- No automatic publication or hackathon submission.
